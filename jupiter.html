the jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass one-thousandth that of the Sun, but two-and-a-half times that of all the other planets in our Solar System combined. Jupiter has been known since ancient times and is named after the Roman god Jupiter. Jupiter is composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. It has a diameter of 142,984 km (88,846 mi) at its equator, making it the largest planet in our Solar System. Its mass is 318 times that of Earth, and its volume is 1,321 times greater than Earth's. Jupiter's atmosphere is made up mostly of hydrogen (90%) and helium (10%), with trace amounts of methane, ammonia, water vapor, and other compounds. Jupiter has 79 known moons, including four large Galilean moons discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610: Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto. These four moons are among the largest objects in our Solar System other than planets or dwarf planets. Jupiter also has a faint ring system composed mainly of dust particles ejected from its moons by impacts from asteroids and comets. Jupiter rotates faster than any other planet in our Solar System; its day lasts only 9 hours and 55 minutes. Its axial tilt is relatively small compared to most other planets; it rotates almost perfectly upright on its axis with an inclination of only 3° to its orbital plane. This means that seasons on Jupiter are very mild compared to those on Earth; there are no extreme seasonal changes like those experienced here on Earth due to its distance from the Sun. Jupiter's magnetic field is 14 times stronger than Earth's; it extends out into space for millions of kilometers creating a vast magnetosphere around the planet which protects it from solar wind particles and cosmic rays coming from outside our Solar System. The magnetic field also creates auroras near Jupiter's poles similar to those seen near Earth's poles but much more intense due to Jupiter's larger size and stronger magnetic field. Jupiter emits more energy into space than it receives from the Sun; this energy comes from within due to gravitational contraction as well as heat generated by friction within its core caused by its rapid rotation rate. This makes Jupiter one of only five bodies in our Solar System known to generate internal heat (the others being Earth, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune). The Great Red Spot on Jupiter is an anticyclonic storm which has been observed for centuries; it measures approximately 40 000 km across making it large enough to contain two or three planets the size of Earth! The storm moves slowly around Jupiter at about 250 km/h (155 mph) while winds inside can reach speeds up to 400 km/h (250 mph). Jupiter can be seen with binoculars or a small telescope as a bright "star" in our night sky; however it appears as a disk when viewed through larger telescopes due to its large size relative to other objects in our night sky such as stars or galaxies. With larger telescopes one can observe details such as cloud bands or even some features such as storms like The Great Red Spot! Jupiter has an average distance from the Sun of 778 million km (483 million mi). Its orbital period around the Sun is 11.86 years and it rotates on its axis once every 9 hours 55 minutes 29 seconds. The tilt of its axis relative to its orbital plane is 3°, which means it experiences seasons like Earth does but on a much longer cycle due to its greater distance from the Sun. Jupiter has 79 known moons, including four large moons called Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto that were discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610 using his telescope. These four moons are sometimes referred to as "the Galilean satellites". In addition to these four moons there are many smaller moons orbiting Jupiter as well as several rings composed mostly of dust particles and ice chunks ranging in size from micrometers to meters across. The atmosphere of Jupiter consists mostly of hydrogen (90%) and helium (10%) with trace amounts of methane, ammonia, water vapor and other compounds present in small amounts. The upper atmosphere contains clouds made up mostly of ammonia crystals with some water droplets mixed in at higher altitudes where temperatures are lower than -100°C (-148°F). The clouds are arranged into bands or zones parallel to Jupiter's equator with lighter colored zones alternating with darker colored belts or bands running east-west across Jupiter's face when viewed from Earth or space probes such as Voyager 1 & 2 or Galileo spacecrafts that have flown past or orbited around it during their missions over time since 1979 through 2003 respectively.. The Great Red Spot on Jupiter's surface was first observed by Robert Hooke in 1664 using his telescope but may have been seen by earlier astronomers before him as well since it appears to be an ongoing storm system on Jupiter's surface that has been present for centuries if not longer according to some estimates based on observations made over time since then up until now.. The interior structure of Jupiter consists mainly of a rocky core surrounded by liquid metallic hydrogen with some helium mixed in at higher pressures further outwards towards its outer layers where temperatures are cooler than those found at greater depths within it.. Its magnetic field is about 20 times stronger than Earth's magnetic field due largely to its rapid rotation rate combined with its large size which generates strong electrical currents within it.. This strong magnetic field helps protect Earth from cosmic radiation coming from outside
